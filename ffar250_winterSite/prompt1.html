Making Turon (From Memory)

This is a recipe for making turon, a Filipino sweet. Because this is mostly based on memory (and asking family about certain steps), it might turn out a bit awkward, but Iâ€™m sure it will still be tasty!

This desert makes me nostalgic, as I ate a lot of it at home and at festivals when I was a kid. Whenever I think of homemade food, turon is one on the first things to pop into my head.

Ingredients

  • 1 Banana
  • 4 Spring Roll Wrappers
  • Sugar
  • Brown Sugar
  • Water or 1 Egg
  • Vegetable Oil

Directions

  1. Cut the banana in half both vertically and horizontally. There should be 4 banana pieces now.
  2. Place the banana pieces in the middle of the 4 spring roll wrappers.
  3. Dump 1 tablespoon of sugar onto each of the bananas.
  4. Wrap the banana by joining the four corners of the wrapper together and join them with either water or egg yolk.
  5. Prepare a heated pan with vegetable oil and deep fry the turon.
  6. Remove the turon from the pan once they turn golden brown.
  7. Leave 1 tablespoon of oil in the pan and mix it with brown sugar.
  8. Dump all the turon in the pan again and mix them with the brown sugar.
  9. Remove the turon from the pan.
  10. Enjoy!
